3.2.3 Identify System Features:
The System features were determined based on gathered data to ensure
that it aligns with the project objectives. The features of IrriGIS: Geographic
Information System-Based Irrigation Canal Monitoring and Reporting Platform
for the NIA and IAs in General Santos City are the following:


(Mobile Application)

1. Accounts
Provides secure access to the mobile application through email-
password authentication, as well as Facebook or Google account login
options. Users using NIA institutional email are granted unlimited
access, while non-NIA users, the members of Irrigators Associations
(IAs), are prompted to provide their address during registration. This
information is used to assign them to their respective Irrigators
Association. Accounts enable role-based permissions, granting users
different levels of access based on their designated roles within the
system.
2. Geo-Camera with Prompt Report Form
Enables users to capture geo-tagged photos alongside a brief
report form to input image description of an activity performed on the
canal or a report of the canal’s status. Users can input descriptions based
on parameters such as water level, water flow, silt level, and debris.
The report form uses a descriptive rating scale to assess three key
parameters: water level, silt level, and debris level, each rated from 1 to
5. Water level refers to the height of water in the canal, ranging from 1-
31
Dry (no water) to 2-Low (below normal), 3-Normal (ideal operating
level), 4-High (close to overflow), and 5-Overflow (overflowing beyond
capacity). Silt level evaluates sediment buildup, with 1-Clean (no
sediment), 2-Light (minimal accumulation), 3-Normal (moderate
sediment typical for operation), 4-Dirty (substantial accumulation), and
5-Heavily Silted (critical levels impeding flow). Debris level measures
obstructions from waste or natural materials, starting at 1-Clear (no
obstruction), 2-Light (minimal debris), 3-Normal (manageable debris),
4-Heavy (significant blockage), and 5-Blocked (severe obstruction
preventing flow). The selection of a parameter rating is left to the user's
discretion, allowing them to evaluate and report conditions based on
their observation. Additionally, there are presets available. With the
default preset, putting all parameters to 3-Normal. Cleaning preset for
when documenting maintenance and cleaning activities, automatically
setting the Silt & Debris level parameters to 1 for clean and clear. Severe
preset, for when all parameters are set to 5 - Overflow, Heavily Silted,
and Blocked. An additional field for inputting remarks is also provided.
The data will then be sent, organized, and displayed to the report ticket
system.
3. Integrated Report Ticket System
Provides a simplified interface for users to view submitted
reports. The system organizes reports, displaying essential information
such as the submission date, time, location, and a brief description.
Users can track the status of their tickets, as the progress are being
displayed in a timeline. The status progress includes “Pending” where
32
report submissions are awaiting acknowledgment from the admins, “In
Progress” where the report is now acknowledged by the admins and is
awaiting actions from the admin, and “Closed” where the ticket is closed
as it is now resolved. Comments are available for tickets marked as “In
Progress”, providing additional details about ongoing actions.
Additionally, users can update and edit their own submitted reports.
Using the coordinates and details from the geo-tagged images, the data
is also displayed on the GIS Map.
4. Integrated GIS Map
Serves as the core visualization platform, displaying data derived
from the reports. Includes specific interactive maps of irrigation canal
networks overseen by different IAs under the Sarangani-General Santos
- South Cotabato Irrigation Management Office (SCSIMO). The chosen
map to be displayed is specified depending on the user account, where
IAs are geo-fenced depending on their assigned location while NIA
officers have the ability to choose map depending on their current
location. The map displays canal statuses, maintenance activities, and
flagged issues, with geo-tagged photos and metadata displayed through
clickable points. However, the map only displays reports marked as “In
Progress”. The interface provides legends, a brief explanation of the
meaning of the symbols, colors, and styles used on the map.
5. Notifications
Keeps users informed about updates and actions related to their
submitted reports and system activity. Notifications are delivered
including alerts for status changes in the Integrated Report Ticket
System, such as when a report transitions from Pending to In Progress
or when additional comments are added.



(Web GIS – Admin Dashboard)

1. Accounts
Provide admins secure access to the web application through email-
password authentication. Users using NIA institutional email are granted
full access, while non-NIA users, the assigned admins of Irrigators
Associations (IAs), have limited access.
2. Exploded GIS Map
Provides administrators with an advanced and detailed view of the
irrigation canal network by overlaying data layers specific to maintenance
activities, flagged issues, and canal statuses. Unlike the mobile version, the
exploded GIS map allows for multi-layer viewing, enabling the display of
overlapping data sets such as water levels, debris accumulation, and
structural integrity. Administrators can filter layers to isolate specific data
types or focus on particular canal segments. Geo-tagged photos submitted
from the mobile application are linked to corresponding map points,
allowing admins to view associated metadata and reports.
3. Report Ticket System
The Report Ticket System equips administrators with tools to efficiently
manage and resolve reports submitted through the mobile application.
Reports are displayed in a detailed tabular format, including submission
date, reporter information, location, and ticket status. Administrators can
sort and filter tickets based on urgency, category, or location, ensuring
efficient prioritization. The system supports expanded ticket statuses,
including Pending, In Progress, and Closed, with each status accompanied
by a timeline logging updates and actions taken.
When the report submitted from the mobile application is received, it is
initially assigned the “Pending” status, where administrators review and
acknowledge the received submission. Once acknowledged, the report is
marked as In Progress, and administrators take charge of coordinating
necessary actions to address the issue. This may involve dispatching
equipment, approving financial assistance, or deploying additional labor
resources to the affected site. Administrators actively monitor the progress
of each ticket, updating the system with detailed logs of the actions taken to
maintain transparency and accountability.
Finally, the report is marked as Closed once all required actions are
completed and the issue is resolved. Administrators ensure thorough
documentation at each stage, facilitating systematic management of tickets
and providing a clear record of resolutions for future reference. This
structured approach allows administrators to maintain oversight and
efficiently manage the lifecycle of all reports.
4. History
Serves as a comprehensive database that organizes all past recorded
activities and closed reports related to the irrigation canals. Administrators
can access detailed documentation of geo-tagged submissions, including
metadata such as date, time, location, and user details. Historical trends and
recurring patterns, such as frequent issues in specific locations, are
highlighted through graphical summaries.
5. Secure User Management System
Manages user accounts and their associated roles, ensuring secure access
and role-specific permissions for both mobile and web platforms.
Administrators can add, edit, or deactivate accounts, assigning roles such as
IA Members and NIA Field Officers for the monitoring role, or NIA Admins
for the administrator role, each with predefined access levels and privileges.
6. Notifications
Provides administrators and personnel with a centralized system for
receiving alerts and updates related to the irrigation network and submitted
reports. Notifications include alerts for reports from the mobile application.
