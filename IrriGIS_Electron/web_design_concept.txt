UI/UX Critique & Improvement Concepts
1. The Reports Panel (Figure 3.25)

The Flaw: The main table is cramped. The "Details" view is poorly organized—the map and photo are just floating on the right, and the timeline text at the bottom is disconnected from the rest of the data.

The Fix: * Upgrade to a modern Data Grid with a clean white background, subtle row borders, and hover effects.

For the Details view, use a Two-Column Card Layout. The left card holds the text details and the vertical timeline (reusing the timeline concept from your mobile app). The right card holds the media (the photo and a mini-map).

2. The History Panel (Figure 3.26)

The Flaw: The table has too much empty horizontal space. When you click a location, it takes you to a new page that is mostly empty except for a giant "BRGY. CONEL" button and a single row of data. This is a lot of clicks for very little information.

The Fix: * Merge this into a single, robust Historical Data Grid. Instead of clicking to a new page to see a specific Barangay, add a Filter Bar at the top of the table. Users can just select "Katangawan" from a dropdown to filter the table.

Add a column for "Resolution Remarks" so admins can see how it was fixed at a glance.

3. The Users Panel (Figure 3.27)

The Flaw: The huge red "Deactivate" buttons draw too much attention; they look like the primary action of the whole page. The modal (popup) for confirming deactivation is completely unstyled and lacks a dark overlay (backdrop) to focus the user's attention.

The Fix:

Add User Avatars (circles with initials or profile pics) next to the names to match the mobile app's feel.

Hide the destructive "Deactivate" action inside a Three-Dot Menu (ellipsis) at the end of the row, or make it a subtle red outline button.

Build a proper Modal Dialog for the confirmation with a dimmed background overlay, a clear warning message, and styled "Cancel" and "Confirm" buttons.
